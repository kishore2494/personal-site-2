## Quick Guide: Build Your Own Local AI Server to Run AI Models Privately

This blog post is inspired by a YouTube video by NetworkChuck, where he builds a local AI server named Terry to run AI models locally on his own hardware.

Running AI models on the cloud can be expensive and raises privacy concerns. By building your own AI server, you can gain more control over your data and how it's used.

Here's a quick guide to get you started on building your own local AI server based on the video:

1. **Choose a Computer**: Any computer you have lying around will work, although a beefier machine will make the AI models run faster.
2. **Install Ollama**: Ollama is the foundation for running AI models locally. Download it from the Ollama website.
3. **Install Additional Software (Optional)**: If you have a GPU, you might need to install additional Nvidia CUDA drivers.
4. **Set Up the Ollama Admin Panel**: This allows you to control who can access the AI server and what models they can use.
5. **Create Model Files**: Model files are used to restrict access to specific AI models on the server.
6. **Install Automatic1111**: This lets you use stable diffusion to generate images with your AI server.
7. **Add Documents to Open Web UI**: This lets you process documents using AI models on your server.
8. **Install the Obsidian Chatbot Plugin**: This lets you chat with an AI model directly in your Obsidian notes application.

Building your own AI server is a great way to experiment with AI and keep your data private. However, it does require some technical knowledge.
