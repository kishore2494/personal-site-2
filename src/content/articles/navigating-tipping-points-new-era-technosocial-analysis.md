## THE GREAT PROGRESSION 2025–2050: Peter Leyden's "Turn-of-the-Wheel" Thesis — A 360° Briefing

It seems we're shifting gears from the persistence of individual existence to the grand sweep of societal and technological transformation! Let's delve into Peter Leyden's perspective, which frames our current era as a period of profound, world-historic change, much like pivotal moments in the past. Think of this as a detailed account, presented in a structured yet conversational manner, of the technological and societal forces at play.

We are currently situated in an **extraordinary moment in history, specifically around 2025**, where **world-historic, game-changing technologies are beginning to scale**. This isn't just incremental progress; it's a period marked by "tipping points," where a gradual, sometimes clunky development suddenly accelerates into something universally adopted and essential, much like the transition from early internet concepts to the widespread necessity of the iPhone.

Peter Leyden, a long-time observer of technology and its societal evolution since the early days of *Wired Magazine* in the 1990s, identifies **three such world-historic tipping points unfolding right now**. His prior work, including the "The Long Boom" cover story at *Wired*, demonstrated his focus on forecasting the future trajectory of digital revolutions and globalization.

## Historical Cycles of Reinvention: The 80-Year Pattern

Leyden argues that America, and the world, experiences **huge societal "contortions" during these junctures**. These periods of significant transformation are not unprecedented in American history, tending to occur in **approximately 80-year cycles**, each marked by **bursts of widespread innovation lasting about 25 years**.

Let's look at the previous instances to understand the pattern:

- **The Post-World War II Boom (1945–1970):** Eighty years prior to our current moment, America emerged from World War II. This followed the "dismantling of the old world" and the "Great Depression of the 1930s," where the existing economic system was failing. This period was characterized by **super polarization** in American society, including an "America First movement" and near "violent conflict". However, it transitioned into what's known as the "great post-war boom," or the "high point of global capitalism," lasting 25 years. This era saw:

◦ **Dramatic economic shifts:** Such as taxing the wealthy at 90%.

◦ **Massive public infrastructure investment:** Including the interstate highway system and the building of suburbs.

◦ **Expansion of education:** Through initiatives like the GI Bill, significantly expanding higher education for the Baby Boomer generation.

◦ This period, encompassing the "Great Society," represented a radical departure from the preceding system.

- **Post-Civil War Progress (1865–1890):** Going back another 80 years, 1865 marked the end of the American Civil War. This was a time of extreme political conflict and high passions, representing a fierce defense of the old system of slavery against the burgeoning manufacturing economy that required free labor. Despite (or perhaps because of) this intense conflict, the post-Civil War period witnessed an **"unbelievable explosion of progress" for 25 years**, including:

◦ **Land redistribution:** The Homestead Act, offering 150 acres for free to those who moved West.

◦ **Educational expansion:** The establishment of land-grant universities to educate ordinary citizens and advance scientific understanding in agriculture.

◦ **Technological infrastructure:** A massive build-out of approximately 175,000 miles of rail, effectively stitching the continent together. America was "reinvented" in just 25 years.

- **The American Founding and Enlightenment (1787–1815):** Another 80 years back, we find the period of America's creation, from 1787 to around 1810–1815. This was part of the broader Western European Enlightenment, a **fundamental system change from a feudal, church-dominated society to what we recognize as the modern world**. This era involved the invention of **six foundational concepts** that continue to underpin our world today. America, with its vast continent, played a key role in the forward motion of innovation from the West during this time.

## The Three Current World-Historic Transformations

Leyden posits that we are now experiencing "one more crank of that wheel" with the arrival of three fundamental, game-changing technologies that signal a new "age" — a threshold from which there is no return. These are not mere improvements; they are **breakthroughs representing a "step-change in our abilities"**.

### 1. Artificial Intelligence (AI):

◦ The arrival of **generative AI, exemplified by ChatGPT 3.5 in November 2022**, is considered a "world-historic moment" and the "starting gun of what will be understood as the age of AI".

◦ AI is projected to lead to an **"explosion of amplification" of our mental powers**, akin to how mechanical engines (like those powered by steam) amplified physical powers, creating the prosperity and wealth of the current world.

◦ Crucially, AI is described as an **energy source that is 100% technology, not a commodity**. This distinction is vital because, unlike coal or oil which must be dug up, a technology's cost can be consistently driven down.

### 2. Clean Energy:

◦ This domain similarly benefits from being a **technology, not a commodity**.

◦ There's a consistent **cost reduction rule in manufacturing**: doubling the production of items like solar panels leads to approximately a **20% drop in cost**. This indicates that the cost of clean energy will "keep getting cheaper".

◦ Battery technology, fundamental to clean energy and electric vehicles, is also undergoing rapid advancements, with new generations like solid-state batteries on the horizon. The "forward motion of costs coming down on clean energy is just beginning," promising **abundant clean energy**.

### 3. Bioengineering (CRISPR):

◦ Around 15 years ago, roughly coinciding with early generative AI breakthroughs, the **CRISPR breakthrough** occurred, enabling **cheap and easy genome editing of any living thing**.

◦ A compelling example is the ability to grow **actual meat cells in a vat** from cow cells, providing the same amino acids and nutrients as a grazing cow, resulting in identical-tasting meat.

◦ The cost of understanding the human genome has plummeted dramatically: from **$3 billion and 15 years in 2003** (the first time humans fully sequenced it) to **$1,000, then $100, and is projected to reach $50 or less**. This enables the cheap and easy **design of biological outcomes**.

## Building a 21st-Century Civilization: The Societal Shifts

These technological foundations are not merely about gadgets; they are the bedrock upon which a **"21st century civilization" can be built**. Leyden posits that these technological shifts necessitate and enable profound systemic changes beyond technology itself.

- **Economic System Shift:** We may be at the beginning of a shift from **"financial capitalism,"** which has primarily served the top 10% (and especially the top 1%) but has not been working for 80% of the population, towards **"some version of a sustainable capitalism"**.
- **Political System Shift:** The world is potentially moving from **"representative democracy,"** a historical breakthrough, towards **"digital democracy"**.
- **Global Governance Shift:** There's also an anticipated move from the era of **"nation states"** (which were an improvement over past empires and colonies) towards **"some kind of global governance"** necessary to coordinate the projected 10 billion people on the planet.

Leyden emphasizes that these are the levels of change that are currently unfolding and that future generations will "wrestle with". Understanding the scale of this impending reinvention is crucial for navigating the future successfully.
